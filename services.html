 Chief Financial Officer [CFO]

Def. CFO: Chief Financial Officer, primarily responsible for managing the financial risks of the corporation. Also responsible for financial planning and record-keeping, as well as financial reporting to higher management.

We are able to perform interim and part-time CFO roles for companies in need of our experience and knowledge, but without the expense of a full time CFO. Our CFO services are designed to support smaller entities that require access to an experienced finance professional on an ad-hoc basis. Flexible and tailored arrangements allow for as much or as little financial assistance as required.

 Tax Compliance

We do tax returns. With the constantly changing tax laws, we are committed to maintaining your tax affairs and ensuring you achieve the minimum tax cost available to your particular circumstances.

Michael and his team remove the stress of dealing with Australian Taxation Office investigations, reviews and negotiations. We are also able assist with State Revenue Office matters.

We prepare statutory accounts too. We ensure all your financial reports provide clean, clear and concise information within the framework of the latest accounting standards. More importantly, we take the time to actually explain how to read your financial reports and how to use them.

 Bookkeeping

One of the biggest frustrations with running a business is having to deal with all the processing of invoices, receipts, statements and so on. A bookkeeper can help you by giving you the time to focus on your business.

We work closely with your bookkeeper or one of our highly experienced bookkeepers can do the work for you. Our bookkeepers are available to work at your business premises if desired.
